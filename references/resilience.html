Resilience

On Resilience Itself

"Resilience is not a fixed trait that some have and some don't. It's fixed processes, tools - all you need to be is someone willing to give them a go"

The American Psychological Association's research confirms that resilience is not an innate personality trait but a set of learnable behaviours, thoughts, and actions -- ordinary people routinely demonstrate resilience when given the right tools and support. — Ann Masten, Ordinary Magic: Resilience in Development (2014)

"Not pursuit of happiness, pursuit of resilience"

"You build resilience by getting better at feeling bad"

Facing Difficulty

"For something to get better, you need to accept that it sucks"

"Helpless is a shitty feeling, but it's not forever"

"Sometimes you need to lose"

"You learn best and grow the most when there's struggle"

In Crisis

"Who do you want to be in a crisis? Someone focusing on what matters, rallying the troops, putting on a brave face, making a difference and taking steps. Not the person who crumbles, complains, adds to the problem."

"Sometimes a finish line is a recovery, not a win"

On Comfort

"The more comfortable you are, the more opportunity life has to make you uncomfortable. Being uncomfortable expands the situations for you that find contentment"

Nassim Taleb's concept of "antifragility" goes beyond resilience: some systems don't just withstand stress but actually improve because of it -- like bones that strengthen under load or immune systems that need exposure to pathogens to function properly. — Nassim Nicholas Taleb, Antifragile (2012)

"Sitting with discomfort is like hugging the cactus. If you hug it long enough, it stops hurting."

"No challenge, no change"

On Rock Bottom

"The feeling that stimulates a turn-around is what makes rock bottom rock bottom. Not circumstances, not location, not loss. It's our 'no more' limit that makes the bottom the bottom." — Tim

On Comfort Zone

"Expand your comfort zone circle so it envelops aspects of future you." — Tim

When Things Are Hard

"All you have to do is get through the day. If you can't do that, get through the next hour, if struggling with that, get through this moment. Because that's where it's won."

"When it's hard, just do ten more. When you get there, tell yourself that you just did ten, so why not ten more"

"Choose your hard"

"A struggle to survive makes your future further away." — Tim

Mindset Shifts

"Good things hit Teflon, bad things hit Velcro"

Psychologist Rick Hanson calls this the brain's "negativity bias" -- the brain is like Velcro for negative experiences and Teflon for positive ones, a survival mechanism that served our ancestors but now skews our perception of reality. Deliberately "savouring" positive experiences for 15-30 seconds helps install them in long-term memory. — Rick Hanson, Hardwiring Happiness (2013)

"Bitter or better"

"Setbacks happen fast. Recovery happens slow."

On Finding Strength Through Adversity

"It's weird that it took me being pulled to weakness to refind my strength." — Tim

"When I have something to fight for, I'm a hell of a fighter." — Tim

"Remember where you draw your strength. And breathe. And keep going." — Tim

"This will only make me stronger. I'm following through on what I said I'd do. I'm being a man of my word." — Tim

"I think the fact that this has affected my work, my concentration, my motivation, my confidence, is the biggest thing that is getting to me." — Tim

The deepest professional and personal challenges reveal that strength isn't a constant -- it needs to be rediscovered. The process of being pulled to weakness can paradoxically reconnect you with your fight.

On Self-Encouragement

"There's a part of me that is looking forward to the challenge. Right this second, that part is being massively overshadowed by anxiety, but my best self can win." — Tim

"I should adapt. And get back to being my best self." — Tim

"Here's to doing my best tomorrow, taking some steps forward, and remembering who I am." — Tim

On Leading When You're Running on Empty

"It's frustrating when you need to be the one to pump up tires, when yours are dead." — Tim

"I was unimpressed... all in all, I was flat. And others were flatter." — Tim

The hardest leadership moments aren't when everything is falling apart externally -- they're when you're falling apart internally and still need to show up for others. This is where character lives.

On Coping With Waiting

"I hate waiting. At least now I've sorted the [issue], though I have questions. More waiting." — Tim

"It isn't fun having it up in the air." — Tim

The inability to affect an outcome is the root of much anxiety. The antidote is not passivity but managed action: sort what you can, hand off what you can't, and trust the process you've built.

On the Cathedral Mindset

"Cathedrals in the Middle Ages took hundreds of years to build. A labour of love and faith, that you'd train and work your whole life knowing full well that you'd never see it finished." — Tim

That's what resilience looks like at the civilisational scale. Building something you won't see completed. Showing up not for the result, but because the work itself is the point. If you can hold this mindset for your own life -- that you're building something worth more than one lifetime's seeing -- the daily frustrations shrink.

Roman Krznaric calls this "cathedral thinking" -- the practice of planting seeds for projects that will bear fruit far beyond our lifetimes, arguing that the decline of long-term vision is one of the defining crises of modern civilisation. — Roman Krznaric, The Good Ancestor (2020)

On Adaptation and Stress

"Adaptation is a product of stress. 1,000 uncomfortable hours is worth more than 10,000 comfortable hours." — Tim

"You get weaker if you don't have an opposing force that's as strong as you." — Tim

The comfortable path doesn't make you stronger. It's the resistance -- physical, emotional, professional -- that forges capability. Without opposition, muscles atrophy. So do mindsets.

On Forgiveness and Strength

"Forgiving someone isn't giving them anything. It's giving yourself something." — Tim

"You relinquish control not because you are powerless, but because you are powerful." — Tim

"It will take more than a bullet to the brain, heart, back, and balls to stop me." — Tim

Forgiveness is a resilience tool, not a weakness. Holding onto resentment is like gripping the blade (see Hold the Knife by the Handle). Letting go gives you back the energy to fight what actually matters.

On Grief

"We need to grieve. We need to go with it. It's like a tidal wave, and we get swept away. You can't really fight it. You need to go with it as best you can. Yes, you need to do what you can to keep your head above water. But you can't just will it away." — Tim

Resilience isn't the absence of grief. It's the willingness to be swept by the wave and still surface. Grief is a tidal force -- you don't fight the ocean, you learn to swim in it.

On Gatekeeping Suffering

"There's a lot of gatekeeping around suffering. Like, is it OK for you to suffer?" — Tim

Nobody gets to audit your pain. The comparison game -- "others have it worse" -- doesn't help. Your struggle is yours, and it deserves to be taken seriously.
