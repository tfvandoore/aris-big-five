Seasons of Life

The Pattern of History

"Good times create weak people. Weak people create bad times. Bad times create strong people. Strong people create great times."

This is both a pattern of history and a pattern of your history as a human.

This cyclical pattern aligns with the "Strauss-Howe generational theory," which proposes that history moves in recurring ~80-year cycles of four "turnings" -- High, Awakening, Unraveling, and Crisis -- each shaping the character of the generation that lives through it. - William Strauss & Neil Howe, The Fourth Turning (1997)

Life's Seasons

Humans recognised the seasons - plant in spring, have enough for winter. Then we had communities, scale, etc. Now think about that for your own life:

| Age | Season | Characteristic | |-----|--------|----------------| | Pre-20 | Spring | You grow without effort | | 20-40 | Summer | Time of explosive growth | | 40-60 | Autumn | Reaping time (if you planted and grew well) | | 60+ | Winter | Reflection and wisdom |

The Timing Principle

"If you plant in the winter you get no reward."

There's a timing to things. Understanding which season you're in helps you make better decisions about effort, risk, and investment.

The Cycle

"Hard times create value investors, value investors create good times, good times create growth investors, and growth investors create hard times. Repeat ♾"

On Knowing Your Season

"Being more ahead in the future often means being less ahead now." - Tim

"We are all temporarily abled. We will all become disabled at some point. So enjoy being abled." - Mark Manson

If you're in your summer, don't rest. If you're in your autumn, don't panic that growth has slowed. If you're in your winter, know that the spring that follows will be informed by everything you endured.

The mistake is treating every season the same. The person who plants in winter gets no reward. The person who rests in summer wastes their explosive growth window. Know your season. Act accordingly.

Erik Erikson's stages of psychosocial development describe a similar progression: each life stage presents a unique developmental challenge (identity in adolescence, generativity in midlife, integrity in old age), and failing to meet the right challenge at the right time creates lasting psychological tension. - Erik Erikson, Childhood and Society (1950)

Time and Hope

"Time vs hope. When one is limited and the other unlimited." - Tim

In youth, time feels unlimited and hope feels small. In maturity, hope can be vast but time feels short. The seasons shift what's scarce and what's abundant. Wisdom is calibrating your actions to whichever is running out first.

Laura Carstensen's "socioemotional selectivity theory" demonstrates that as people perceive their time horizon shrinking, they shift priorities from knowledge-seeking and network-building toward emotional meaning and deep relationships -- explaining why older adults often report higher life satisfaction than younger ones. - Laura Carstensen, A Long Bright Future (2009)
