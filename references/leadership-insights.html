Leadership Insights

Taking Ownership

"Everything your subordinates do right is their fault. Everything they do wrong is your fault, for lack of training, resourcing, or not defining the mission clearly."

"On any team, in any organization, all responsibility for success and failure rests with the leader. The leader must own everything in his or her world. There is no one else to blame." — Jocko Willink & Leif Babin, Extreme Ownership (2015)

"I'd never ask any of my team to not have fear, but I will ask you to contain it now"

"Leadership: 90% uptime, 10% I can't do it"

Giving Responsibility

"You either give someone a responsibility or you don't"

"Tell me what you need to get the job done. But don't let it be balls, or self belief - that's what you need to bring. You can't borrow mine."

On Feedback

"Seek criticism. Often it's those who don't want to hurt us are the ones who have the best insight"

"The more personally you take criticism the more likely it is true"

"Radical candor is what happens when you put 'care personally' and 'challenge directly' together." The best feedback comes from people who care enough to be honest. — Kim Scott, Radical Candor (2017)

"I think I'm letting you down. I'd like your input on where I can improve"

"Always ask why you get this feedback, how can I become self-servicing here"

Communication

"Truth without timing falls on deaf ears"

"It isn't just being a straight shooter. When you shoot straight, defenses go up, listening stops. What we need to do is get the message across."

"I'll tell it how I understand it, you then tell me how I have it wrong"

"Swear at the situation, not the person"

On Teams

"Think how you'd feel if someone in your favourite sports team gave 50% and said it's 'just a team'. That's how I feel if someone says 'it's just a job'."

"We had a situation of the vocal blind leading the knowledgeable silent"

"Management is pruning. Editing."

Self-Leadership

"Self leadership is about having a vision for yourself. Inspire yourself, don't wait for someone else to do it. Support yourself, have empathy - no one knows who you are trying to be, and the associated struggles, better than you."

"Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is our freedom and power to choose our response. In our response lies our growth and our freedom." Self-leadership begins with recognising that space. — Viktor Frankl, Man's Search for Meaning (1946)

On Effort & Standards

"Effort is the universal currency of respect. The problem arises when you think you're working hard, but you're objectively missing the mark." — Tim

"Don't do your best, do what it takes. Sometimes your best just needs to be better. If you just say 'well, I tried my best' and give yourself a pat on the back, you'll never be able to do what's required." — Tim

"Excellence is appreciated in every culture, and excellence is understanding of nuance." — Tim

"I'm giving you these comments because I have very high expectations, and I'm confident you can meet them." — Tim

Research by Cohen, Steele, and Ross (1999) found that prefacing critical feedback with the statement "I'm giving you these comments because I have very high expectations and I know that you can reach them" dramatically increased receptiveness and effort, particularly among those who might otherwise distrust the feedback. — Cohen et al., Journal of Experimental Social Psychology (1999)

On Difficult Conversations

"What would you tell me if you knew I wouldn't be upset or angry?" — Tim

"Come on, this isn't you. I know you. You are..." — Tim

"You've always been upfront with me, and now I suddenly feel like you're not." — Tim

"The others look up to you. You're a role model. When you talk about others, it has an impact. They watch you, they listen to you. You need to keep it all above the line." — Tim

"Be mature, direct and firm." — Tim

On Influence & Mindset

"It isn't unusual when people leave for them to say something to make themselves feel better, to justify it all. But it can get in people's heads and be detrimental." — Tim

"She has poisoned your mindset. You need to stand guard at it." — Tim

"You don't throw people under the bus. But you do indicate that the bus is around." — Tim

"Leadership is communicating to people their worth and potential so clearly that they are inspired to see it in themselves." — L. David Marquet

On Character

"We are who we are when no one is watching." — Tim

"It's the difference between character and reputation." — Tim

"Weakness of attitude becomes weakness of character." — Einstein

"Character, in the long run, is the decisive factor in the life of an individual and of nations alike." — Theodore Roosevelt. C.S. Lewis put it similarly: "Integrity is doing the right thing, even when no one is watching." The distinction Tim draws between character and reputation aligns with what John Wooden called "the true test of a man's character" -- what he does when no one is watching. — John Wooden, Wooden on Leadership (2005)

"You need three things: the balls to start, the brain to learn, and the heart to never quit." — Tim

On Vulnerability and Strength

"I'd never ask any of my team to not have fear, but I will ask you to contain it now." — Tim

"I almost broke last week... My normal coping mechanisms haven't been working. But I am just doing what I need to do. Step by step, day by day." — Tim (journal reflection on leading through personal adversity)

Tim reflects that bottling the "pissed off-ness" — channelling anger with love, alignment, and in the interest of the greater good — is a leadership superpower. The key is not being afraid to enforce standards, even at the cost of friendships. "If I don't, I won't be as valuable as I could have been, and the future for my family will be limited."

On Power and Control

Reflections from the ConradV episode: "I gave away a lot of my formal power instead of sharing it, or keeping one hand on it. I hid behind the 'you must let managers manage' trope and didn't step in. But I didn't step in because I felt powerless myself. When I gave my formal power, I let my personal power become weaker. So when I needed to be strongest, I had allowed myself to become weakest." — Tim

"It was when I had one of those 'that's enough' moments that I took action. I sorted it, and found I was stronger than I thought." — Tim

On Playing the Game

"Play the game. Play the game of being the best you can be. To get through a day while staying on task. To make more of a difference. To play you must be entitled to lose. That's what makes it a game. That's what makes it fun." — Tim

On Resilience in Leadership

"I need to be pissed off more often. That if I can bottle the pissed off-ness and use it when needed, with love, alignment, and in the interest of the greater good, I could be on to something." — Tim

"I'm too flexible on my standards, afraid to speak up. Afraid I'll lose my friends. But if I don't, I won't be as valuable as I could have been." — Tim

Recognised Sources

"Not finance. Not strategy. Not technology. It is teamwork that remains the ultimate competitive advantage, both because it is so powerful and so rare." — Patrick Lencioni, The Five Dysfunctions of a Team (2002)
