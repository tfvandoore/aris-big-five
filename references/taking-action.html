Taking Action

Action Over Everything

"Actions beat intentions"

"A pro just gets in to it whether they want to or not"

"It's action that fuels motivation. It's action that creates inspiration"

Behavioural activation research in psychology confirms that action precedes motivation, not the other way around. People who wait to "feel like" doing something rarely begin, while those who start despite low motivation consistently report improved mood and drive after beginning. - BJ Fogg, Tiny Habits (2019)

"The biggest lie we tell ourselves is that if circumstances were different, we would be different. No you wouldn't. If you won't do it now, you wouldn't do it then. Action leads to change, not the other way around."

Starting

"Starting is hard, continuing is easy"

"You can edit bad writing, but you can't edit a blank page"

"Starting tomorrow is a tomorrow that never comes"

The 49/51 Principle

"You might not want to do something, but it could be 51% of you doesn't want to, and 49% does. If you're accountable to someone, they give you a small prod and the 49% wins."

"Sometimes 49% wants to do something and 51% doesn't. It doesn't take much to kick that over to action."

On Courage

"Courage doesn't feel good"

"Not less scary, more courage"

"You don't know how strong you are until you push yourself against an impossible challenge"

The Courage Cycle

"Commit, courage, capabilities, confidence - in that order." - Tim

The project you're not ready for right now? Commit to it first. The commitment forces the courage. The courage produces the capabilities. The capabilities build the confidence. Most people wait for confidence to arrive before committing. It doesn't work that way. Confidence is the output, not the input.

Albert Bandura's research on self-efficacy demonstrates that confidence (self-efficacy) is primarily built through "enactive mastery" - doing the thing - rather than through preparation or positive thinking. The act of performing successfully is the strongest source of future belief in one's capability. - Albert Bandura, Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control (1997)

"How you feel is how you feel, but how you act is a choice"

The Three Stages

"Get going. Get good. Get smart." - Tim

The order matters. You don't get smart first and then start. You start messy, develop competence through repetition, then the wisdom follows. Most people try to reverse the sequence and never leave the gate.

On Backbone Over Wishbone

"The wishbone has nothing without the backbone." - Tim

Wishing is easy. The spine to follow through is what separates dreams from results.

On Outcomes Over Intentions

"Life is measured by outcomes, not intentions." - Tim

"Doubt can only be removed by action." - Tim

"The most effective way to do it is to do it." - Amelia Earhart

"If you wait until you have time to start something important, you'll stop as soon as your time gets crunched. If something is important, make the time." - Tim

Intentions feel productive because they activate the same brain regions as action. But the scoreboard doesn't track what you planned. It tracks what you did.

Research on "goal substitution" shows that announcing intentions publicly can actually reduce follow-through, because the social acknowledgment gives a premature sense of completion - the brain treats the plan as if it were the achievement. - Peter Gollwitzer, "When Intentions Go Public," Psychological Science (2009)

On DCA in Life

"DCA in your personal life. Keep the faith, keep doing the right things when you're down. Good times roll, but the bad times need faith. Like binge eating vs dieting - DCA health. At regular intervals exercise, eat healthily, regardless of how we feel. Like investing regardless of how we feel. Put in the input, regardless of how we feel. Keep the averages, the run rate, up." - Tim

Dollar cost averaging isn't just an investment strategy. It's a life philosophy. Show up at regular intervals. Put in the effort regardless of how you feel. The average input over time is what compounds.

James Clear frames this as the "aggregation of marginal gains" - tiny improvements of 1% per day compound to make you 37 times better over a year. The key is not intensity but consistency: showing up on schedule rather than waiting for inspiration. - James Clear, Atomic Habits (2018)

On Talk vs Action

"There's a shared pool of energy between talk and action." - Tim

Every unit of energy you spend talking about what you're going to do is a unit you didn't spend doing it. The pool is finite.
