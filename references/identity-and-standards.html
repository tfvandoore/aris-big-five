Identity and Standards

What is Identity?

"Identity is your self-concept, the values and aspirations to which you're most committed to."

Standards Define You

"Your standards. Low standards means no closing the gap on aspirations. It's a show of your commitment."

Key Questions

  • How do you want to make people feel?

On Confidence

"Outwork your self doubt."

"You build confidence by having a stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are."

This aligns with Albert Bandura's concept of "self-efficacy," where confidence is built not through affirmation but through accumulated "mastery experiences" -- direct evidence that you can do what you set out to do. - Albert Bandura, Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control (1997)

The Giant is You (from The giant is you)

Book idea: The art of the forgotten art of perspective.

"We are all giants. To an insect we're massive. To an elephant we're not. But just because elephants exist doesn't mean we aren't giants."

When we compare ourselves against others, we have a neck problem - we only look up. We don't see that we are giants to others. Others we can help up.

We are giants in different ways:

  • Financial giants
  • Lifestyle giants
  • Family giants
  • Health giants

"Accept you are a giant, see yourself as one. And to be a bigger giant you need to be big enough to take responsibility and be small enough to take baby steps in the right direction."

On the Cavern of Comfortable Complacency

"If things are good, good. If things are bad, good -- because it will push you towards making a change. But beware the cavern of comfortable complacency." - Tim

The danger zone is not crisis (which forces action) nor success (which rewards it). The danger zone is the middle -- comfortable enough to avoid pain, not driven enough to pursue growth.

Psychologists call this "satisficing" -- settling for good enough. Herbert Simon's research showed that when the pain of the current situation falls below a threshold, people stop optimising entirely. - Herbert Simon, Models of Bounded Rationality (1982)

The Three-Filter Test

"Be smart enough. Try hard. Don't be a dickhead. If those three are a given, then it's a question of how, not who." - Tim

If someone is intelligent enough, works hard, and isn't an arsehole, you can bridge almost any gap. The problems only start when one of the three is missing.

On Growing With Your Kids

"As parents, we need to keep growing and push ourselves. We want to raise kids we'd be proud to call our friends. But if they do everything we ask and make us proud, but we don't, we won't deserve to have them as friends." - Tim

"If the kids are motivated, fit, focused, and we aren't, it's inevitable they will drift apart." - Tim

"Don't show them that when you have young kids you give up, and then turn it back on as they are less needy. You'll just teach them to not prioritise their kids when they are young." - Tim

The identity you model is the one they inherit. If you read instead of scroll, exercise when they're young, take a deep breath when they test you, the reward of lifetime best mates is in your future. Standards aren't just for you -- they're the textbook your kids are silently reading.

Bandura's social learning theory demonstrated that children learn behaviour primarily through observation and imitation of role models, not through instruction -- making what parents do far more influential than what they say. - Albert Bandura, Social Learning Theory (1977)
